3/16/26

listened to i love my computer by ninajirachi for the first time this week. its a really good album start to finish. love the energy. my favorite song on the album is either csirac or battery death. its rare that i enjoy an album start to finish these days, but this album really sunk its hooks in me. the production mixes the smooth, calm flows of 2000s trance with the more punchy stabs that i love in modern edm. the songs transition effortlessly into one another in a satisfying way. keeps the energy up. the lyrics are almost disconcertingly relatable. the nostalgia for adolescencence in the 00s mixes with complex emotions of growing up in the information age. i was thinking about infohazard today. as i was listening to the song, i was turning over in my head whether it was in good taste or not. it is a dance oriented song about a snuff film she stumbled onto when she was using the computer as a child. ultimately there is nothing wrong with the song. it is a product of her experience and an expression of her feelings about a complicated trauma in her childhood. expressing oneself through music can be cathartic. that the song has an upbeat tempo does not inherently make it light or positive. electronic music is as viable a medium as any for complex emotional expression, and this topic was handled tastefully. the remixes of some of the other tracks on the album have been excellent too. underscores' remix of its you melts. felt like i was 12 again listening to dubstep for the first time. also s/o horsegiirl, nice groove on the delete remix. if youre reading this go listen to the album god damn
